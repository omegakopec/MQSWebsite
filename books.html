Apollodorus Lost His Bright-Eyed Canary

An existentialist writer and his communist friends take a trip down-South on a dilapidated Chinatown bus to see the solar eclipse. The horrors of the road are both real and psychological, as they confront mysterious madmen and modern anxieties. Though staring into an eclipsing sun can cause blindness, these three companions stand to gain a new level of sight... that is, if they survive the journey.

Sweet Grass and Noxious Poppies
(work in progress)

Four young friends on a road-trip to California make a regrettable pitstop to the vacant post-Woodstock-era town of Willshire, New Hampshire. It’s there that they meet the darkly charismatic music-shop owner, opium addict, and resident cult-leader, Kirk Roades. While rummaging Kirk’s shop, our first-person narrator, Milo, discovers that he can play a strange sci-fi-instrument (known as the Gasoont). Kirk hopes to use Milo’s newfound talent to summon G’znt’ck, a malevolent opium-god with sleeplike powers. All-the-while, Milo reminisces about a girl that he met while traveling Europe, and finds out that she’s here too for some reason.
